Abstract

Indoor localization is a fundamental challenge in robotics and augmented reality applications. Traditional approaches often rely on geometric features or require extensive infrastructure. We propose a novel approach that leverages semantic understanding of floor plans and visual features to achieve accurate indoor localization. Our method combines deep learning-based semantic segmentation with geometric reasoning to match visual observations with floor plan elements. We demonstrate that this semantic understanding significantly improves localization accuracy compared to purely geometric approaches. Our experiments on multiple datasets show that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance while being more robust to environmental changes and requiring less infrastructure than traditional approaches.
